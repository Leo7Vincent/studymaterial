III. Answer the following questions briefly:

1. What is history? What is the origin of the word 'history'?

History is the study of our past, a record of events that happened long ago. The word 'History' comes from the Greek word 'Histo' which means 'know this'.

2. Why do we study history? Give two reasons.

1. History helps us understand our society and culture.
2. History helps us learn from past mistakes so that we don’t repeat them.

3. Which period in history is called prehistoric?

‘Pre-history’ refers to the era when humans had not developed the art of writing, meaning no written records exist.

4. Name two sources of history. What do literary sources include?

Two sources of history are literary sources and archaeological sources. Literary sources include secular and religious literature.

5. Give one example each of religious and secular literature.

Example of religious literature — the Mahabharata. Example of secular literature — Abhigyan Shakuntalam.

IV. Distinguish between:

1. Archaeological sources and literary sources.

Archaeological sources include inscriptions, coins, tools, pottery, etc. Literary sources include religious books, poems, and travelogues.

2. Monuments and inscriptions.

Monuments are old buildings such as temples, forts, palaces. Inscriptions are writings engraved on rocks, pillars, metal plates, etc.

3. Religious and secular literature.

Religious literature is related to a religion (e.g., the Puranas, Jatakas). Secular literature is not related to any religion (e.g., Arthashastra, Indika).
