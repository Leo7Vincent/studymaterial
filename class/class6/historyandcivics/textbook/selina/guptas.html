IV. Answer the following questions:

  1. Question 1. Why is Chandragupta I called the real founder of the Gupta Empire?
    Answer: Chandragupta I is called the real founder of the Gupta Empire because he was first independent and important ruler of this dynasty. He raised the power and prestige of his empire through conquests and marriages. He assumed the title **Maharajadhiraja**, i.e., ‘the king of kings’. He started the Gupta Era in 320 AD which marked the date of his accession.
  2. Question 2. Describe the military achievements of Samudragupta. Which source tells us about him?
    Answer: The **Allahabad Pillar Inscription**, composed by Harisena, serves as the most significant historical record of Samudragupta’s reign. It highlights his military prowess, detailing his victories across northern and southern India. In the north, he defeated nine rulers, while in the eastern Deccan and southern regions, he overcame twelve kings. However, rather than annexing these southern territories, he chose to collect tributes from the defeated rulers, recognizing the challenges of governing distant lands from his capital. This strategy allowed him to amass vast treasures, reinforcing his dominance without direct territorial control.
  3. Question 3. Why is Chandragupta II considered the greatest ruler of the Gupta dynasty?
    Answer: **Fa-Hien’s** account forms the historical source of information about Chandragupta II. Chandragupta II was a great conqueror. His greatest achievement was to destroy the power of the **Shaka** from Malwa, Gujarat and Saurashtra permanently. He gained control over three important ports of Bharuch, Cambay and Sopara. He also assumed the title **Shakari** or ‘the conqueror of the Shakas’. He was a great patron of art and literature. He had at his court, the famous **Navaratnas**, the most learned men of his time. Poet Kalidasa was one of them.
  4. Question 4. What were the religious beliefs of the people during the Gupta period?
    Answer: The Gupta kings were Vaishnavas, but they were tolerant towards all religions. **Brahmanism** had come to the forefront. The two gods who commanded the worship of the majority were Vishnu and Shiva. Religious sacrifices were performed. Hinduism became the most powerful religion and the Gupta kings gave donations for building of temples. Buddhism no longer received the royal patronage, though it continued to be the second most important religion.
  5. Question 5. Describe the great progress made in art and architecture during the Gupta period.
    Answer: Art and architecture gained great heights during the Gupta Period. Many temples were built. The temples at Deogarh, Sanchi and Bhittargaon are very famous. The 29 cave temples at Ajanta near Aurangabad in Maharashtra and Bagh in Madhya Pradesh were also built during this period. The walls of these caves were covered with paintings (called murals or frescoes). Two main centers of art were Mathura and Sarnath. The image of the seated Buddha in a yogic pose is one of the masterpieces of art of this period.
  6. Question 6. With examples prove that Sanskrit literature reached its peak during this period.
    Answer: The Gupta period is called the **Golden Age of Sanskrit literature**. Chandragupta II is credited with maintaining **Nav-Ratna** (Nine luminaries) at his court. Kalidasa and Amarasimha adorned his court. Kalidasa was the greatest and most famous poet of this period. He wrote **Abhijnana Shakuntalam, Raghuvansham, Meghaduta** and **Kumar Sambhava**. Amarsimha wrote **Amarakosa**, a Sanskrit dictionary. Panini was a great grammarian. **Ashtadhyayi**, the greatest work on Sanskrit grammar was written by Panini during this period.
  7. Question 7. Describe the progress made in the field of science during the Gupta period.
    Answer: Scientists, mathematicians, astronomers and physicians flourished in the court of the Gupta rulers. Two well-known mathematicians and astronomers of this period were **Aryabhatta** and **Varahamihira**. Aryabhatta said that the earth moves round the sun and at the same time it moves on its axis. The books authored by Aryabhatta are **Aryabhatiya** and **Suryasidhanta**. Varahamihira wrote **Brihat Samhita**, an Encyclopedia on Astronomy. Indian mathematicians knew the use of **zero**. The Indians also used the **decimal system**.
  8. Question 8. Write a short note on Gupta administration.
    Answer: The Gupta Empire was systematically organized into provinces, each known as a **Bhukti**. These provinces were further divided into districts, referred to as **Vishayas**, which were then subdivided into villages called **gramas**. Governance at each level was carefully structured—**Uparikas** were appointed to oversee the Bhuktis, while **Vishayapatis** managed the Vishayas. To ensure efficient administration, district councils assisted provincial governors in decision-making. The provinces were entrusted to princes, who were supported by officials known as **Kumarmatyas**. At the village level, **Gramapatis** were responsible for local governance, ensuring smooth functioning within their communities. This well-organized system contributed to the stability and prosperity of the Gupta Empire.
  9. Question 9. With appropriate examples prove that the Gupta period has been rightly called a ‘Golden Age’.
    Answer: The Gupta period is known as “Golden Age of Indian History”. The rule of the Gupta kings lasted for more than two hundred years. India made progress in almost every field under the Gupta kings. This period saw considerable achievements in the field of Indian culture especially in the areas of art, architecture and literature. Remarkable advancement also took place in the field of science, medicine and metallurgy. Peace and prosperity prevailed in the empire.

C. Answer the following questions.

  1. Question 1. What was the political condition of India after the decline of the Kushanas and the Satavahaans?
    Answer: After the decline of the Kushans and Satvahanas, India was divided into a number of small kingdoms and republics. The political condition was instable.
  2. Question 2. Why did the Pallava ruler of Kanchi form a confederacy against Samudragupta?
    Answer: The Pallava ruler of Kanchi formed a confederacy because he realized that he could not face Samudragupta alone. So he formed a confederacy of the kings of South India under his leadership.
  3. Question 3. Why did Samudragupta not annex the South Indian kingdoms after defeating them?
    Answer: Samudragupta did not annex the south Indian kingdoms because he realized it would not be easy to control such a vast empire without proper transport and communication facilities.
  4. Question 4. Why did the frontier states of the east and the west surrender to Samudragupta?
    Answer: The frontier states of east and the west surrendered to Samudragupta because the rulers of these states accepted him as their overlord and paid large sum of money as a tribute.
  5. Question 5. What was the extent of Samudragupta’s empire?
    Answer: Samudragupta’s empire extended from Bengal in the east to Delhi in the west, from the Himalayas in the north to Narmada in the south. His indirect control over the vassal states also.
  6. Question 6. How did Chandragupta Vikramaditya strengthen his position through matrimonial alliances?
    Answer: Chandragupta Vikramaditya strengthened his position by matrimonial alliance. He married the daughter of a powerful Naga ruler. He gave his daughter in marriage to the Vakataka ruler in the Deccan from whom he got valuable help during his campaigns against the Sakas.
  7. Question 7. Mention two important reasons for the downfall of the Gupta empire.
    Answer: The important reasons for the downfall of the Gupta empire are following:
    • Skandagupta’s successors were weak.
    • They could not protect the empire from the repeated onslaughts of the Huns.
  8. Question 8. What according to Fa Hien, was the condition of the people during the Gupta period?
    Ans. According to Fa Hien’s account the people were honest, secure, prosperous and happy during Gupta Period. There was no capital punishment, for very serious crimes like treason and rebellion the right hand was cut off. Criminals were usually fined. Taxes were light.
  9. Question 9. What did the Gupta rulers do to encourage education?
    Answer: Liberal grants were given to encourage education and Guptas established Nalanda University.
  10. Question 10. What progress did the Guptas make in the following fields:
    Answer: The Gupta period witnessed remarkable advancements across various fields of science and technology:
    1. Astronomy – Aryabhatta, a renowned astronomer, made groundbreaking discoveries, including the Earth's rotation on its axis and its movement around the Sun. He also accurately explained the causes of lunar and solar eclipses.
    2. Mathematics – The concept of zero and the decimal system were developed during this era, revolutionizing mathematical calculations and laying the foundation for modern arithmetic.
    3. Medicine – Eminent physicians Vagbhata and Dhanvantri contributed significantly to medical science, excelling in surgery and healthcare practices. Their work played a crucial role in shaping early Indian medicine.
    4. Metallurgy – The Mehrauli Iron Pillar, constructed in the 4th century CE, stands as a testament to the advanced metallurgical skills of the time, remaining rust-free for centuries. Additionally, the 25-meter-high copper statue of Buddha and the widespread use of gold coins highlight the expertise in metal casting and craftsmanship.
    These achievements solidified the Gupta era as a golden age of scientific progress and innovation.
