History - Neolithic Age Solutions

Question 10: How did the invention of the wheel transform the life of the people in the Neolithic Age?

The discovery of the wheel was an important achievement in the Neolithic Period. It led to rapid progress in pottery making. The wheel was used in horse-carts and bullock-carts, making transport easier and quicker. It was also used for carrying loads, spinning, and weaving, marking a major step in civilization.

Question 11: Why did early humans make pots? How did the invention of the potter’s wheel affect pottery making?

Early humans made pots to store grains, water, and food. The invention of the potter’s wheel was a major breakthrough, enabling potters to create various shapes and sizes. Pots were often decorated with floral designs and geometrical patterns.

Question 12: ‘Agriculture and domestication of animals brought dramatic changes in humans’ lives’. Explain.

Agriculture: The discovery of agriculture turned humans into food producers, allowing them to lead a settled life. Crops, vegetables, and fruits were grown near their huts.

Domestication: The domestication of animals provided meat, milk, and skins. Dogs, goats, sheep, and cattle were kept, and bullocks, donkeys, and horses were later used for plowing fields.

Question 13: Write short notes on:

The Barter System: A system of exchanging services and goods. For example, potters received food grains in exchange for pots.

Social Life during the Neolithic Age: People lived in groups and formed rules to regulate society. The village head was chosen based on wisdom or strength to maintain order and protect against enemies.
