III. Answer the following questions:

1. What do you mean by ‘Panchayat’?

The word Panchayat literally means a team of five members.

2. How do Panchayats help villagers?

Panchayats solve local problems, provide democracy training, support village welfare projects, and develop administrative skills.

3. What are the advantages of Village Panchayats?

Village Panchayats provide clean drinking water, maintain roads, ensure medical facilities, support primary education, and help in infrastructure development.

IV. Additional Questions:

1. What are the three institutions of the Panchayati Raj?

1. Village Panchayats (Local level)
2. Panchayat Samiti (Block level)
3. Zila Parishad (District level).

2. What is the composition and function of a Block Samiti?

A Block Samiti consists of elected representatives from Village Panchayats, experts, and funding from the government. It supervises village development programs.

3. How is the Zila Parishad formed and what are its functions?

The Zila Parishad consists of representatives from Block Samitis, Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, Vidhan Sabha, and special members. It oversees district development plans.

V. Importance of Panchayati Raj:

1. What has the Panchayati Raj done for rural areas?

It has created political awareness, improved village welfare activities, and drawn government attention to rural problems.

2. What is the relationship between the state government and the Panchayati Raj?

The state government provides financial grants and oversees rural development through Village Panchayats.

F. Answer the following questions briefly:

1. What are the functions of the union, state, and local self-governments?

Union (Central government): Handles national matters like defence, finance, foreign trade, railways, postal services, etc.
State government: Maintains law and order, transport, health services, and community development programs.
Local self-government: Addresses local problems and ensures basic needs for people.

2. What is local self-government? Name the two kinds of local self-governing bodies in India.

Local self-government refers to governance by elected representatives at the community level.
Urban: Includes municipalities, corporations, and cantonment boards.
Rural: Includes Zila Parishads, Block Samitis, and Panchayats.

3. Mention any five compulsory functions of the Gram Panchayat.

1. Provides drinking water, schools, health, and sanitation facilities.
2. Constructs buildings, roads, drains, and tanks.
3. Supports farmers in improving farms and cattle.
4. Takes care of weaker sections of the society.
5. Organizes educational programs, cattle fairs, and village markets.

4. What is the importance of Gram Panchayats?

The Gram Panchayat helps villagers solve daily problems. It carries out administrative, economic, judicial, and social functions, ensuring active participation in governance.
